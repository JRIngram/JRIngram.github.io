About Me

Hey there, I'm Jamie. I'm a student at Aston University studying Computer Science. I enjoy attending hackathons and other social technology events; this lead me to be one of the organisers for AstonHack in 2016, and again in 2018.

Outside of Computer Science, I enjoy reading, playing bass guitar, finding new music to listen, playing badminton and have recently started bouldering. In general, I'd say I have a passion for learning and taking part in new experiences.

Whilst studying at Aston University, most of my learning has been centred around Java, SQL, HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Thus these languages are my strongest areas. I have also managed to gain some experience in PHP and Python. Because of this, I have a strong understanding of Object-Oriented principles.

Throughout projects both at University and side projects in my spare time, I have made considerable use of Git and performed the majority of the programming on Ubuntu, making me somewhat comfortable with both Git and Linux.

Previously I have worked as a Software Developer at Beautiful Canoe. During this time I worked on the Pharmacy Learning Environment, an examination system for Pharmacy students to use which would check they understood pharmacy law and prescriptions. As this was a web based project, my work was heavily focused on web technologies.
